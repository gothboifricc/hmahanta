Study of Influence on Test Scores

An in-depth analysis of student test scores data covering key metrics like Scores by District, Extracurricular Activities etc.


July 30, 2024
Scroll

Information

In this project, I analysed student test scores across districts and states to uncover insights into the impact of extracurricular activities, city and district performance, tutoring hours, and year-over-year reading score improvements.

--- Investigate practices in such top-performing districts and share these strategies with districts showing lower average scores like Magnolia and Pinecrest to improve overall reading scores.


ðŸ”¹Insight 2: Students involved in extracurricular activities like Debate, Music, and Sports show a higher average math score, suggesting a positive impact on academic performance by these activities.

Suggested action based on this insight:

--- Schools wanting to better their math scores should encourage extracurricular participation mainly in these activities as part of their education strategies.

--- I would advise to try to recognise and study the successful methods used in the above mentioned states, then bring in similar programs in the states with relatively minimal changes like Tennessee (~0.63%), Colorado (~0.66%), and Connecticut (~0.86%).


ðŸ”¹Insight 4: The states and cities vary widely in their average math scores, with urban areas like Reno (Nevada State) and Cleveland (Ohio State) achieving higher average math scores, possibly due to better access to resources.

Suggested action based on this insight:

--- Identify differences in resource allocation between these states/cities and consider reallocation or support for underperforming states/cities like San Angelo (Texas State), Knoxville (Tennessee State) etc. to help bridge the gaps.
